# LatinoNZ — Business Overview

> A modern business directory connecting the Latin community in New Zealand.

---

## Business Idea & Opportunity

LatinoNZ is an online business directory platform designed specifically for Latin-owned businesses and professionals operating in New Zealand. The platform allows Latin entrepreneurs to list their services and be discovered by the broader Latin community and the general NZ public.

The opportunity lies in a clear gap: there is no dedicated, modern, Portuguese/Spanish-friendly directory for Latin businesses in New Zealand. Latin immigrants often rely on informal WhatsApp groups and word-of-mouth to find trusted services from people who share their language and culture. LatinoNZ formalises and scales that network.

The current phase is a **waitlist launch** — collecting early sign-ups from business owners across 16 service categories (restaurants, beauty, construction, tech, real estate, etc.) before the full platform goes live.

**Revenue potential (post-launch):**
- Freemium listing model (free basic, paid featured/premium)
- Sponsored placements and category highlights
- Lead generation packages (WhatsApp-direct contact)
- Advertising from NZ brands targeting the Latin demographic

---

## Potential Market

**Primary market:** Latin-owned businesses and self-employed professionals in New Zealand.

**Secondary market:** Latin consumers in NZ searching for culturally familiar services (language, food, customs).

**Tertiary market:** New Zealand businesses wanting to reach the Latin demographic.

**Market indicators:**
- New Zealand's Latin American population has grown steadily, concentrated in Auckland, Wellington, and Christchurch.
- The community is digitally active and heavily reliant on mobile/WhatsApp for communication.
- No direct competitor offers a dedicated, modern, bilingual Latin business directory in NZ.
- The 16 service categories already defined cover the most common Latin business types, indicating strong product-market fit awareness.

## Types & Roles of Stakeholders

**Internal:**
- Founder/Developer — product vision, technical build, business strategy
- Admin (via `/admin` panel) — manages waitlist data, monitors sign-ups, exports leads

**External:**
- Latin business owners — primary users; list their businesses on the platform
- Latin consumers in NZ — end users who search and contact businesses
- Investors/Sponsors — potential future funding or advertising partners
- NZ government/councils — regulatory compliance, potential grants for multicultural initiatives
- Tech partners (Supabase, Cloudflare) — infrastructure and reliability

---

## Business Relationships

- **B2B:** LatinoNZ ↔ Latin business owners (the platform serves them as clients)
- **B2C:** LatinoNZ ↔ Latin consumers (the platform serves them as users)
- **B2B2C:** Businesses listed on LatinoNZ reach consumers through the platform
- **Partnership potential:** Latin cultural associations, NZ multicultural councils, immigration consultants, and Latin media outlets (radio, YouTube channels, Instagram pages)

WhatsApp is already embedded as the primary contact channel between businesses and consumers, which aligns with how the Latin community naturally communicates.

---

## Communication Methods & Strategies

**Current (waitlist phase):**
- Landing page with SEO meta tags (Portuguese-language, NZ-targeted)
- Waitlist modal capturing business name, owner, email, WhatsApp, and category
- Confirmation via WhatsApp and email upon platform launch (promised in success message)

**Recommended channels:**
- WhatsApp broadcast lists — highest open rate in the Latin community
- Instagram/TikTok — visual content showcasing listed businesses
- Email marketing — launch announcements, feature updates
- Word-of-mouth referrals — incentivise early adopters to share
- SEO — Portuguese and Spanish keywords targeting NZ searches
- Partnerships with Latin influencers and community leaders in NZ

**Tone & language:** Portuguese-first (Brazilian community is large in NZ), with Spanish support planned. Warm, community-driven messaging.
